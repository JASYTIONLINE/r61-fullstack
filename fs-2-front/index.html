The Last Gate

Character: Riya Vedanta Backstory

Role: Protagonist

    The Plan-In-Exile
    The Reluctant Leader
​ ​

Riya Vedanta is a former elite PM, burnt out after a catastrophic failure known as GOATSTREAM. As the story unfolds, she is forced back into leadership and must rediscover her purpose — not by following The Plan,
but by becoming it. She was once a rising star in interstellar systems delivery. She was a certified master of procedural governance, fluent in PMBOK 6, and known for executing flawlessly within fixed scope, timeline, and budget.

​

Then came Project GOATSTREAM — a well-documented disaster now used as a cautionary tale in PM onboarding.

​ GOATSTREAM was meant to deliver neural comfort loops to deep-space miners using ambient goat bleats. It followed every PMBOK 6 standard: triple-phase risk review, locked scope, stakeholder matrix, air-tight compliance, and it failed spectacularly.
​ During a routine transport mission: a solar flare disrupted the signal between the Miner Colony and the test goats. The audio over-amplified. The goats panicked and started to stampede through the cargo hold. It was not long before the goats workd them selves intot a frenzy and increased teh feedback loop to thee server. Then the unthinkable happened - Then the goats exploded. ​ Riya could not understand what happened. She had followed the procuders to the letter:
    1. She had done the Risk Analsyis
    2. Logged the changes, and filled out the Gant Chart
    3. The Gate had said GO. She had followed the book.
​

But still it all went to hell. The miners in Sector 9 reported weeks of auditory hallucinations. The only reason she wasn’t spaced was the amount of documentation she’d filed. She wasn’t officially fired. She was buried — reassigned to back-end monitoring, forgotten by leadership, and, over time, by herself.

​

When we meet her, she’s surviving on caffeine and apathy, quietly rotting in her own reputation. But the Plan — the real Plan — still flickers beneath the surface. She can feel it. She just doesn’t believe she deserves to touch it.
